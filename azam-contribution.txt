For my part of the project, I handled the second half of the pipeline, which was Phase 2b through Phase 4. After my partner finished the database schema, ingestion script, and the ticker matching layer, I picked things up from there and built the rest of the system end to end. I wrote fetch_prices.py, which pulls daily stock prices for every matched ticker from Yahoo Finance through the yfinance library along with the S&P 500 benchmark used for our market adjustment. I made sure the fetcher was idempotent using ON CONFLICT DO NOTHING, added retry logic with backoff for transient Yahoo failures, and structured it as one transaction per ticker so a single bad symbol couldn't tank the whole run. That phase ended up writing around 281K daily price rows and 1,666 S&P trading days into the database. Phase 3 was the heaviest piece. I wrote analyze_events.py, which is where the actual event study lives. I implemented log returns, the market adjusted abnormal return calculation, the Cumulative Abnormal Return across our four windows, and the Standardized CAR with a 250 day estimation window. I also wrote the hypothesis testing layer (one sample t test, Patell Z, 1000 sample bootstrap CI), the OLS regression with HC3 robust standard errors, and the Random Forest classifier with 5 fold cross validation. Along the way I had to debug a numpy float64 binding bug with psycopg2, fix a Patsy issue with column names containing dashes, and patch a bug from Phase 1 where percentage_laid_off was never populated because of a column naming mismatch. Phase 4 was visualize.py, where I generated the six figures and four tables that ended up in our final report. Beyond the code, I also wrote the final technical report itself and put together the demo video walking through our results.
